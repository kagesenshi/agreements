This LIMITED LIABILITY PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT  (the "Agreement") is made on this **${EFFECTIVE_DATE}** ("Effective Date")

**BETWEEN:**

The persons whose names, addresses, and details are set out in **Schedule A: Details Of Partners And Capital Contributions** attached hereto (hereinafter collectively referred to as the "Partners" and individually as a "Partner").

**RECITALS:**

1. The Partners desire to form a limited liability partnership under the Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2012 of Malaysia (the "Act") for the purpose of carrying on a business with a view to profit.

2. The Partners wish to set out the terms and conditions governing the relationship among themselves, the management of the LLP, and the conduct of its business.

**IT IS HEREBY AGREED as follows:**

**DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION**

   1. In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires

      a) **"Act"** means the Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2012 and any statutory modification or re-enactment thereof for the time being in force.
     
      b) **"LLP"** means the limited liability partnership registered under the Act pursuant to this Agreement.
     
      c) **"Managing Partner"** means the Partner designated to manage the day-to-day operations and execute contracts on behalf of the LLP, as appointed in Clause 9.
     
      d) **"Registrar"** means the Registrar of Limited Liability Partnerships under the Companies Commission of Malaysia (SSM).
     
      e) **"Confidential Information"** shall mean all non-public, proprietary information, business data, trade secrets, business methodologies, client lists, project information, financial data, technical data, software code, marketing plans, and any other information in any form disclosed or made known to a Partner that relates to the business, products, services, or operations of the LLP or its clients.

**NAME, REGISTERED OFFICE AND BUSINESS**

   2. **Name:** The business of the LLP shall be carried on under the name of **${LLP_NAME}** (the "LLP").

   3. **Registered Office:** The registered office of the LLP shall be at the address set out in **Schedule B: Details Of The LLP**, or such other place as the Managing Partner may from time to time determine.

   4. **Business:** The principal business of the LLP shall be as set out in **Schedule B: Details Of The LLP** and such other business as the Partners may from time to time unanimously determine.

**TERM**

   5. The LLP shall commence on the date of its registration with the Registrar and shall continue until it is wound up or dissolved in accordance with the Act and the provisions of this Agreement.

**CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND SHARE PERCENTAGE**

   6. The initial capital of the LLP shall be **${INITIAL_CAPITAL}** contributed by the Partners in the respective amounts and proportions (the "Share Percentage") as set out in **Schedule A: Details Of Partners And Capital Contributions**

   7. No Partner shall be entitled to any interest on their capital contribution.

   8. Any further capital required for the business of the LLP shall be contributed by the Partners in their respective Share Percentages, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

**MANAGEMENT AND AUTHORITY**

   9. **Appointments and Responsibilities:** The Partners shall assume the specific operational roles, statutory appointments (including the Compliance Officer pursuant to Section 27 of the Act and the Managing Partner), and key responsibilities as set out in **Schedule C: Designation Of Partner Roles And Duties**.

   10. **Exclusive Signing Authority:** Notwithstanding any default provisions in the Act, only the Managing Partner shall have the authority to sign contracts, agreements, deeds, and legally binding documents on behalf of the LLP. No other Partner shall have the authority to bind the LLP in contract, incur any liability, or execute any document on behalf of the LLP without the prior written consent of the Managing Partner. 

   11. **Major Decisions:** While the Managing Partner oversees day-to-day operations, major decisions shall require the unanimous consent of all Partners. These major decisions include, but are not limited to:

       a) Changing the principal nature of the business;
  
       b) Amendment of this Agreement;

       c) Selling, purchasing, or otherwise disposing of or acquiring any asset or assets with a value exceeding **RM 5,000**;

       d) Entering into any contract or commitment involving an amount exceeding **RM 10,000**;

       e) Incurring debt exceeding **RM 10,000**; or
 
       f) Admitting new partners.

   12. **Routine Meetings:** The Partners shall hold at least one (1) meeting every three (3) months for the purpose of reviewing the LLP’s operational and financial performance and planning.

       a) **Calling of Meetings:** A meeting of the Partners may be called by the Managing Partner or any two (2) Partners by giving not less than **seven (7) days’** written notice to all other Partners. The notice must specify the time, date, location, and the general purpose of the meeting.

       b) **Quorum:** A quorum for any Partner meeting shall be the physical or virtual presence of Partners holding a simple majority of the total ownership interest. No business may be transacted at a meeting unless a quorum is present.

   13. **Voting.**

       a) **Routine Decisions:** All routine operational and administrative decisions shall be decided by a simple majority vote of the Partners present at the meeting.

       b) **Major Decisions:** Decisions concerning matters designated as major decisions in Clause 11 (including, but not limited to, changing the principal business, incurring significant debt, or admitting new partners) shall continue to require the unanimous written consent of all Partners.

       c) **Voting Rights:** Each Partner shall be entitled to one (1) vote on any matter put to a vote.

**FINANCIAL MATTERS AND REMUNERATION**

   14. **Financial Year:** The financial year of the LLP shall end on the **${FINANCIAL_YEAR_END}** in each year.

   15. **Bank Accounts:** All funds of the LLP shall be deposited in its name in such bank account(s) as shall be determined by the Managing Partner.

       a) **Banking Representatives:** The bank account shall be operated by the representatives of the LLP detailed in **Schedule C: Designation Of Partner Roles And Duties**.

       b) **Authorized Signatories:** The Banking Representatives shall be designated as Authorized Signatories to the bank account(s) and shall have the authority to sign cheques, make withdrawals, effect transfers, and transact any other banking business on behalf of the LLP, in accordance with the limits and conditions set forth in this Agreement or as otherwise agreed by the Partners.

       c) **Withdrawal Limits:** Any single withdrawal, transfer, or transaction exceeding **RM 10,000** shall require the joint signatures of two (2) Authorized Signatories. Withdrawals, transfers, or transactions equal to or less than **RM 10,000** may be executed by a single Authorized Signatory.

       d) **Change of Signatories:** The Partners may, from time to time, resolve to change the Banking Representatives and Authorized Signatories, and such change shall be effective upon written notification to the relevant bank(s) and amendment of **Schedule C: Designation Of Partner Roles And Duties**.

   16. **Entitlement to Variable Remuneration:** It is expressly agreed that Partners actively engaged in the daily operations, management, execution of client deliverables, or administrative functions for the LLP ("Working Partners") are entitled to receive remuneration. To ensure such remuneration is wholly and exclusively incurred in the production of the LLP’s income, it shall be variable, commensurate with the actual services rendered, and strictly contingent upon the successful realization of corresponding client revenue.

   17. **Basis of Remuneration Calculation:** The variable remuneration for each Working Partner shall be calculated strictly based on the agreed rate detailed in Schedule D: Rate Card.


   18. **Claim, Cleared Funds Contingency, and Verification Process:** It is a strict condition precedent that no remuneration may be claimed until the LLP has successfully received cleared funds from the respective client.

       a) **Standard Claims:** To facilitate payment, a Working Partner must submit a formal Internal Claim Record to the Managing Partner within one (1) month after the relevant client payment has fully cleared into the LLP’s bank account. This record must be substantiated by objective evidence (e.g., timesheets, milestone reports, or internal invoices) and proof of the cleared client transaction.

       b) **Managing Partner Claims:** Where the claimant is the Managing Partner, to maintain internal controls and eliminate conflicts of interest, the Internal Claim Record must be explicitly linked to objective third-party triggers (such as accepted client invoices or executed software deliverables). All such claims must be reviewed and countersigned by at least one other Partner prior to disbursement. This countersignature serves exclusively as verification that the corresponding third-party client funds have cleared the LLP’s bank account, and not as a subjective assessment of performance.

   19. **Cash Flow Dependency and Limitations:** Notwithstanding Clauses 16, 17, and the clearance of client funds under Clause 18, the physical disbursement of accrued remuneration remains strictly subject to the LLP possessing adequate operational cash flow to meet its other primary liabilities. Remuneration shall be paid exclusively from the gross income of the LLP and shall under no circumstances be drawn from the capital contributions of the Partners. In the event operational cash flow is prioritized for other LLP expenses, accrued remuneration shall be legally deferred and recorded as an amount payable to the respective Partner until sufficient surplus funds are available.

   20. **Tax and Statutory Treatment:** All parties acknowledge that payments made under this Clause 16 constitute remuneration for active services rendered wholly and exclusively in the production of the LLP’s income. These payments are entirely distinct from the distribution of LLP profits. The LLP shall treat these payments as deductible business expenses, and the receiving Partner is solely responsible for declaring this remuneration as income for personal taxation purposes.

   21. **Statutory Contributions and Partner Treatment:** The Partners acknowledge and agree that, pursuant to Malaysian law, specifically the Employees Provident Fund Act 1991, the Employment Insurance System Act 2017, and the Social Security Organisation Act 1969, Partners, including Working Partners, are generally treated as owners/principals of the business and are not considered "employees" of the LLP for the purpose of mandatory statutory contributions (EPF, SOCSO, EIS).

       a) **EPF, SOCSO, EIS Non-Applicability:** The LLP shall therefore not be required to make mandatory contributions for any Partner to the Employees Provident Fund (EPF), Social Security Organisation (SOCSO), or Employment Insurance System (EIS) based on the remuneration paid under this Clause 16, as such payments are for services rendered by a Partner to the LLP, not wages paid to an employee.

       b) **Partner Responsibility:** Each Partner is solely responsible for managing their own personal retirement savings, social security coverage (e.g., voluntary contributions to EPF, or coverage under self-employment schemes), and income tax compliance related to the remuneration and profit distributions received from the LLP.

   22. **Profits and Losses:** Any residual net profits or losses of the LLP at the end of the financial year, after accounting for the monthly remuneration and tax provisions, shall be divided among or borne by the Partners in proportion to their Share Percentages, unless otherwise agreed.

**INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY**

   23. **Retention of Intellectual Property:** Every Partner shall absolutely retain all ownership, rights, title, and interest in and to their respective Intellectual Property.

   24. For the avoidance of doubt, "Intellectual Property" includes but is not limited to patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, business methodologies, software, concepts, and designs, whether pre-existing the formation of this LLP or developed independently by a Partner during the term of this Agreement.

   25. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as transferring the ownership of any individual Partner's Intellectual Property to the LLP or to any other Partner. Should the LLP require the use of a Partner's Intellectual Property, it shall be strictly subject to a separate, explicit licensing agreement agreed upon by the IP-owning Partner.

   26. **LLP-Specific Intellectual Property:** Notwithstanding Clause 23, where Intellectual Property (including, but not limited to, software code, client documentation, or custom methodologies) is created by a Partner specifically for the purpose of fulfilling the LLP's client obligations or for use in the LLP's core business operations, the creating Partner shall automatically and without further formality grant the LLP a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide, sublicensable, and unrestricted license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and publicly display such Intellectual Property solely for the business purposes of the LLP. The Partner retains ownership, but the LLP is ensured the necessary rights to operate its business.

   27. **Brand and Trademarks:** All corporate identities, trading names, domain names, logos, slogans, trademarks, service marks, and goodwill associated with the business of the LLP, whether registered or unregistered, shall be the sole and exclusive property of the LLP. Any Partner who creates, develops, or registers such assets shall hold them in trust for the LLP, and shall execute all necessary documents to legally vest ownership in the LLP upon request. These assets shall not be considered the separate Intellectual Property of any individual Partner under Clause 23.

**CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE**

   28. **Confidentiality:** Each Partner agrees to treat the Confidential Information of the LLP and its clients as strictly confidential and shall not, without the prior written consent of the Managing Partner:

        a) Disclose the Confidential Information to any third party.

        b) Use the Confidential Information for any purpose other than in the necessary and proper conduct of the LLP’s business.

        c) Make copies of any Confidential Information unless necessary for the proper performance of their duties as a Partner.

   29. **Post-Termination Obligation:** The obligations of confidentiality under this Clause 28 shall survive the termination of this Agreement and shall continue for a period of one (1) year after a Partner retires, is expelled, or otherwise ceases to be a Partner of the LLP.

   30. **Exceptions:** The obligations of confidentiality shall not apply to any information that:

       a) Is or becomes publicly known through no wrongful act of the receiving Partner.

       b) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or a valid order of a court or regulatory authority, provided that the Partner shall give the Managing Partner prompt written notice of such requirement prior to disclosure.

       c) Was already in the Partner's possession prior to the execution of this Agreement, as evidenced by written records.

**NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION**

   31. **Restrictions:** A Partner shall not, directly or indirectly, throughout the term of this Agreement and for a period of one (1) year after ceasing to be a Partner (the "Restricted Period"):

       a) Engage in any business activity competing with the LLP's Principal Nature of Business in Malaysia;

       b) Solicit business from any client of the LLP; or

       c) Solicit, entice, or induce any existing Partner, Working Partner, employee, or independent contractor of the LLP to terminate their relationship or to be employed by or contract with the departing Partner or associated entity.

   32. **Exception:** Notwithstanding clause 9.1, the restrictions set out in clauses 9.1(a), 9.1(b), and 9.1(c) shall not apply if there is prior written unanimous agreement from all remaining Partners waiving the application of the relevant restriction(s) for a specific instance or period.

**INDEMNIFICATION AND LIMITATION OF LIABILITY**

   33. **Indemnification of the LLP:** Each Partner (the "Indemnifying Partner") agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the LLP and the other Partners from and against any and all claims, liabilities, losses, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) that arise from:**

       a) The Indemnifying Partner's gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud in the conduct of the LLP's business.

       b) Any material breach of the Indemnifying Partner's duties, obligations, or representations under this Agreement.

       c) Any action taken by the Indemnifying Partner outside the scope of their designated authority.

   34. **Indemnification of Partners:** The LLP shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless any Partner from and against any claim or liability incurred solely as a result of such Partner acting in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interest of the LLP, in the ordinary course of the LLP's business, provided such action does not constitute fraud, gross negligence, or willful misconduct by that Partner.

   35. **Limitation of Internal Liability:** No Partner shall be liable to the LLP or any other Partner for any loss, damage, or claim arising from errors in judgment, mistake of law or fact, or any action or inaction taken in good faith for the LLP's benefit, except to the extent that such liability arises from the Partner's willful misconduct, fraud, or gross negligence.

   36. **Limitation of External Liability:** The liability of each Partner for the debts and obligations of the LLP shall be limited to the extent provided in the Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2012 (Malaysia). No Partner shall be personally liable for any obligation of the LLP solely by reason of being a Partner, nor shall a Partner be personally liable for the wrongful act or omission of any other Partner or employee of the LLP.

**RETIREMENT, DEATH, OR EXPULSION**

   37. **Retirement:** A Partner may retire from the LLP by giving not less than 90 days' written notice to the other Partners.

   38. **Condition on Retirement:** Should a Partner's retirement result in the Limited Liability Partnership consisting of only one remaining Partner, the retiring Partner shall be obligated to, in good faith, assist the remaining Partner in identifying and introducing a suitable new Partner to join the LLP prior to the effective date of retirement.

   39. **Death or Bankruptcy:** In the event of the death or bankruptcy of a Partner, the LLP shall not be dissolved. The deceased or bankrupt Partner's share shall be dealt with in accordance with the Act, and their estate/assignee shall be entitled to receive their prevailing capital account balance and accrued unpaid remuneration, but shall not have any right to interfere in the management of the LLP.

   40. **Valuation of Partner's Share upon Cessation:** Upon the death or bankruptcy of a Partner, the value of the deceased or bankrupt Partner's share for the purpose of distribution shall be limited to their prevailing capital account balance and accrued unpaid remuneration, excluding any valuation of the LLP's underlying assets and goodwill, as determined by the LLP's most recent audited accounts or, if no such accounts are available, a statement of accounts prepared by the Managing Partner as of the date of cessation, which determination shall be final and binding on all parties.

   41. **Transfer Restrictions:** A Partner's interest in the LLP is personal and temporary, and no Partner may sell, assign, transfer, charge, encumber, or otherwise dispose of their "Share Percentage" or any right in the LLP's capital, assets, or profits to any third party, whether during life or upon death, with any such attempt being void. A Partner wishing to leave the LLP must do so solely via retirement or, if applicable, expulsion. The LLP will not admit a person as a Partner based on a purported assignment of a former Partner's interest; new Partners are admitted only by unanimous consent of all existing Partners.

   42. **Expulsion:** A Partner may be expelled by the unanimous vote of the other Partners for gross misconduct, material breach of this Agreement, or any action that brings the LLP into disrepute.

**WINDING UP**

   43. The LLP may be wound up voluntarily by the unanimous agreement of all Partners, or in the case of death and there's only one partner left, by the remaining partner.

   44. Upon winding up, the assets of the LLP shall be applied first in discharging its debts and liabilities, and any surplus shall be distributed to the Partners in proportion to their respective Share Percentages.

**GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION**

   45. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Malaysia.

   46. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or invalidity thereof, shall first be attempted to be resolved through good faith negotiations. If such dispute cannot be resolved within thirty (30) days, it shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration in Malaysia in accordance with the Arbitration Act 2005. The costs of mediation, if any, shall be borne equally by the parties. Unless otherwise determined by the Arbitrator, each party shall bear its own attorney fees, but the Arbitrator may award costs of the arbitration, including the Arbitrator’s fees, to the prevailing party. The arbitration shall strictly comply with the provisions of the Arbitration Act 2005 and its subsequent amendments or re-enactments. The seat of the arbitration shall be Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

   47. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be illegal, invalid, or unenforceable under the laws of Malaysia, such provision shall be deemed severed from this Agreement, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
