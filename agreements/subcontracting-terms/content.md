These General Subcontracting Terms and Conditions (the "Subcontracting Terms" or "Agreement") establish the framework under which the party designated as the prime contractor ("Prime") and the party designated as the subcontractor ("Subcontractor") (collectively referred to as the "Parties" and individually as a "Party"), shall cooperate for the performance of services. These Subcontracting Terms are incorporated by reference into the primary agreement between the Parties or any Statement of Work (SOW) executed hereunder.


1. **STRUCTURE**

   a) This Agreement shall be read together with, and if they conflict will follow precedence according to the following:

      i) The specific terms entered in the applicable Statement of Work (SOW) or Order Form;

      ii) The specific modifications entered in the incorporating document;

      iii) The specific terms entered in the primary subcontractor agreement between the Prime and Subcontractor;

      iv) These General Subcontracting Terms and Conditions.

2. **DEFINITIONS**

   a) "**PROJECT**" shall mean the services and Deliverables to be provided to Prime or a customer of Prime under a specific SOW.

   b) "**DELIVERABLES**" shall mean any and all items described in a SOW that Subcontractor agrees to deliver to Prime or to Prime's client in performance of the services governed by such SOW.

   c) "**SOW**" shall mean a statement of work which references this Agreement and defines, with respect to a specific Project, the services to be performed, the Deliverables to be provided, Prime Responsibilities, final or interim project completion dates or milestones, and fees or rates. A SOW signed by both parties shall be incorporated in and made a part of this Agreement. In the event of a conflict between the SOW and this Agreement, the terms of the Agreement shall prevail.

3. **COLLABORATION**

   a) **Good Faith and Teamwork.** The Parties, along with any other subcontractors or partners engaged in the Project, shall cooperate in good faith and maintain open communication to ensure the successful, integrated delivery of the Project and the satisfaction of the Prime's customer.

   b) **Inter-Subcontractor Collaboration.** Subcontractor acknowledges that the Project may require integrated efforts with other subcontractors or vendors designated by Prime. Subcontractor agrees to collaborate effectively with such other subcontractors, sharing necessary technical interfaces, dependencies, and relevant Project information to facilitate seamless execution of the overall Project.

   c) **Joint Problem Solving.** The Parties, together with any relevant other subcontractors, commit to prompt sharing of Project information, and joint problem-solving to address any issues or delays that may arise during the performance of the Work.

4. **SERVICES**

   a) Prime will coordinate all activity with respect to its customers, which may involve the use of Subcontractor personnel for performance of services ("Work"). Subcontractor shall perform the Work and provide the Deliverables to Prime set forth in the applicable SOW.

   b) Each of Prime and Subcontractor shall appoint a project manager who shall be responsible for coordinating the activities under an SOW. Each party shall direct all inquiries, requests and reports concerning the Work and Deliverables to the other party's project manager. Subcontractor shall submit written progress reports, and Prime shall submit written replies, in accordance with a schedule to be determined by the project managers.

   c) Time is of the essence in the performance of the Work and other obligations hereunder, and Subcontractor agrees to complete the Work by the milestones and dates set forth in the SOW. If Subcontractor fails to complete the Work in a satisfactory manner by the date specified in the SOW, fails to complete any specified portion of Work by the milestone (if any) provided for in the SOW, or fails to make reasonable progress toward satisfactorily completing Work by the date specified in a SOW which does not specify milestones, except for delays by Prime, Prime may, at its option and in its sole discretion:

      i) defer all payments payable under the SOW until Subcontractor is in compliance with all performance and delivery requirements related to that Work and, in the case of Work under a SOW that does not specify milestones, demonstrates to Prime's reasonable satisfaction that Subcontractor will complete the Work by the date specified; or

      ii) notify Subcontractor that the SOW will be terminated unless Subcontractor cures all breaches hereof and, if the SOW does not specify milestones, demonstrates to Prime's reasonable satisfaction that Subcontractor will complete the Work in a satisfactory manner by the date specified, all within a period of ten (10) days. Unless Subcontractor timely complies with this requirement, at the end of such ten (10) day period, Subcontractor will discontinue performance under the SOW, except that Subcontractor will deliver to Prime all Deliverables under the SOW, whether or not completed, and if after inspecting the same, Prime does not promptly return them but instead notifies Subcontractor of its election to retain such Deliverables, it will pay Subcontractor in accordance with the applicable payment and reimbursement provisions, with no obligation to make such payments if Prime elects to return such Deliverables to Subcontractor and certifies that Prime has retained no copies.

5. **FEES AND PAYMENT**

   a) The fees due to Subcontractor for services in connection with a Project, exclusive of taxes, shall be as set forth in the applicable SOW. Any expenses, including reasonable travel expenses, incurred by Subcontractor and necessary to perform the services that are pre-approved by Prime and in accordance with Prime's or customer's travel policy, a copy of which shall be provided to Subcontractor prior to any authorized travel on behalf of Prime, will be reimbursed by Prime. Prime shall pay sales, excise and similar taxes arising in connection with services for a Project, except for any income tax due on income received by Subcontractor, upon receipt by Prime of an invoice from Subcontractor setting forth a description and amount of such taxes.

   b) Subject to Section 4(c) above, payment for all Work performed by Subcontractor in connection with a Project shall be made by Prime to Subcontractor in accordance with the payment schedule and terms set forth in the applicable SOW.

6. **CHANGE OF SCOPE**

   a) Subcontractor will notify Prime in writing whenever it identifies the need to perform Work or provide a Deliverable additional to or different from those set forth in a SOW (a "Change of Scope"). Prime may notify Subcontractor in writing whenever Prime believes there is a need for a Change of Scope.

   b) If Prime wishes Subcontractor to perform or deliver what is identified in a Change of Scope provided by Subcontractor, Prime will so notify Subcontractor in writing. Subcontractor will provide an estimate of the cost and schedule impact of performing or delivering the Change of Scope, which estimate will be provided within a mutually agreed time frame. Subcontractor shall not be obliged to take further action with respect to the Change of Scope until Subcontractor and Prime execute an appropriate written amendment to the applicable SOW.

   c) If Prime fails to meet the Prime responsibilities as defined in the SOW, and such failure materially and adversely affects Subcontractor's costs or schedule or precludes further work by Subcontractor on the Project until the Prime Responsibilities are met, then Subcontractor will notify Prime in writing, and Subcontractor and Prime will promptly cooperate to make an appropriate written amendment to the applicable SOW.

7. **TERM AND TERMINATION**

   a) This Agreement is effective upon execution for a term of one year from the date first written above, or until the completion of all Projects described in any SOW executed hereunder, whichever period is longer. The parties may extend the term by mutual written agreement.

   b) This Agreement may be terminated by either party at any time upon thirty (30) days written notice to the other party; provided, however, any such termination will not excuse the nonperformance of either parties' obligations with respect to any unfinished Work (or payment for such Work) and completion of all Projects under a SOW or pursuant to survival clauses.

   c) Prime shall have the right to terminate a SOW at any time by giving written notice to Subcontractor, in which case (unless Subcontractor is in breach of this Agreement), Prime will: 
      i) pay Subcontractor for the reasonable value of the Work performed prior to receiving such notice of termination, which value shall be calculated as follows: (A) for a SOW based on time and materials, the hourly rates for Subcontractor personnel as may be set forth in the applicable SOW under which Work was actually performed, (B) if the SOW provides for interim payments for partial completion of the Work upon reaching a milestone, the portion of the next milestone payment amount that reflects Prime's reasonable estimate of the pro rata portion of the Work necessary to reach the next milestone which was actually performed by Subcontractor after the prior milestone, if any, or (C) for a SOW other than those described in (A) and (B) above, the total fees due under the SOW, as equitably adjusted for that portion of the Work that has not yet been performed; and 
      ii) reimburse Subcontractor for all reasonable reimbursable expenses under Section 5 above incurred by Subcontractor prior to receiving such notice of termination and all reasonable costs incurred by Subcontractor thereafter to return or dispose of unused materials and equipment acquired by Subcontractor to perform the Work or deliver the Deliverables under such SOW, provided that Subcontractor will exert its best efforts to minimize such costs. 
      iii) In the event of such a termination, Subcontractor shall deliver to Prime, or to its customer if Prime so directs, all copies of any and all materials or information provided by Prime or Prime's customer; and Deliverables created by Subcontractor for Prime or Prime's customer hereunder, whether complete or partially complete.

8. **CONFIDENTIALITY**

   a) It is anticipated that each of the parties will disclose to the other confidential information. "Confidential Information" means any information obtained from or through Prime or Prime's customer, or developed or obtained by Subcontractor in connection with the performance of this Agreement or any SOW hereunder, including, without limitation, software programs, technical data, methodologies, customer information and business information of the parties, and any information contained in any Deliverable.

   b) Each party shall be a "Disclosing Party" with respect to Confidential Information which that party discloses to the other and shall be a "Receiving Party" with respect to Confidential Information which that party receives from the other. A Disclosing Party shall not identify as Confidential Information any information, which the Disclosing Party does not, in good faith, consider to be proprietary and/or confidential.

   c) The Receiving Party shall employ diligent efforts to maintain the secrecy and confidentiality of all Confidential Information. Such diligent efforts shall be at least equivalent to that degree of care which the Receiving Party normally exercises with regard to its own property that it maintains secret and confidential, but in any event no less than a reasonable degree of care. The Confidential Information may be disclosed only for purposes of the joint activity with the Disclosing Party and only to the Receiving Party's employees with a need to know, provided that each such employee has previously been advised of the terms of this Agreement. The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party to employees of a customer with a need to know for purposes of the joint activity of the parties hereunder, only pursuant to a written confidentiality agreement of the Receiving Party and the customer which (i) expressly identifies the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, (ii) provides no less protection of the Confidential Information than the provisions of this section, and (iii) is, in all other respects, reasonably acceptable to the Disclosing Party. The foregoing notwithstanding, Prime shall have the right to disclose pricing information of Subcontractor to Prime's customer or potential customer, to the extent Prime reasonably determines that such disclosure is appropriate in the course of its negotiations with its customer.

   d) The disclosure of Confidential Information shall not be construed to grant to the Receiving Party any ownership or other proprietary interest in such information. The Receiving Party agrees that it does not acquire any title, ownership, or other intellectual property right or license by virtue of such disclosure.

   e) Confidential Information shall not include any information disclosed hereunder which: 
      i) was rightfully in the Receiving Party's possession before receipt from the Disclosing Party other than through prior disclosure by the Disclosing Party; or 
      ii) is or becomes a matter of general public knowledge through no breach of this Agreement; or 
      iii) is rightfully received by the Receiving Party without an obligation of confidentiality and from a third party who did not receive it directly or indirectly from the Disclosing Party; or 
      iv) is independently developed by the Receiving Party; or 
      v) is disclosed under operation of law, governmental regulation, or court order, provided the Receiving Party first gives the Disclosing Party notice and a reasonable opportunity to secure confidential protection of such information.

   f) Upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall: 
      i) immediately cease using the Confidential Information, 
      ii) promptly return to the Disclosing Party or, at the Disclosing Party's direction destroy, all tangible embodiments of the Confidential Information, and 
      iii) promptly certify in writing the Receiving Party's compliance with this paragraph.

9. **INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY**

   a) **Background IP.** Each Party shall retain all rights, title, and interest in and to any intellectual property, materials, tools, routines, methodologies, or data owned or developed by such Party prior to the Effective Date of this Agreement or independently of the Work performed under any SOW ("Background IP"). Neither Party acquires any rights in the other Party's Background IP, except as expressly provided herein. If Subcontractor incorporates any of its Background IP into the Deliverables, Subcontractor hereby grants Prime (and Prime's customer) a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, display, and distribute such Background IP solely as embedded in or necessary for the use of the Deliverables.

   b) **Foreground IP.** Unless otherwise explicitly agreed in a SOW, all new intellectual property, works of authorship, inventions, products, or processes created, conceived, or reduced to practice specifically for Prime or Prime's customer by Subcontractor in the direct performance of the Work under this Agreement ("Foreground IP") shall be the exclusive property of Prime (or Prime's customer, as Prime may direct). Subcontractor hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such Foreground IP to Prime. Subcontractor agrees to execute any documents reasonably necessary to perfect such assignment.

   c) **Third Party Software.** Subcontractor may use third party, Open Source Software or Proprietary Software to render the Services and Deliverables ("Third Party Software"). Intellectual Property Rights of Third Party Software are subject to the license granted by the respective owners of the Third Party Software. Subcontractor shall provide a list of Third Party Software to be used and their respective licenses within the applicable SOW. Subcontractor represents that any Third Party Software incorporated into the Deliverables complies with their respective licenses and will not impair Prime's or Prime's customer's rights to use the Deliverables as intended.

   d) Subcontractor shall furnish to Prime, or to its customer if Prime so directs, copies of all drawings, plans, specifications, reports and data developed or produced for Prime under this Agreement.
   
10. **INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY INDEMNITY**

    a) Prime will notify Subcontractor in writing of any claim, action or proceeding ("Infringement Claim") against Prime or Prime's customer that any Deliverable or other work product produced by Subcontractor for Prime, or the use thereof, infringes a patent, trademark, copyright or other proprietary right of a third party or misappropriates a trade secret of a third party.

    b) Upon being notified of any Infringement Claim brought against Prime or Prime's customer based on such a claim, Subcontractor, at its sole cost, shall indemnify and defend Prime and Prime's customer in said action, perform any negotiations for settlement or compromise of the action, and pay any and all settlements reached and/or costs and damages awarded in any such action, together with reasonable attorney's fees; provided, however, that to the extent that any action is based upon a claim that material furnished to Subcontractor by Prime or Prime's customer, or the use thereof, infringes a patent, trademark, copyright, or other proprietary right of a third party, or misappropriates a trade secret of a third party, Prime, at its sole cost, shall indemnify and defend Subcontractor in such action, perform any negotiations for settlement or compromise of the action, and pay any and all settlements reached and/or costs and damages awarded in the action, together with reasonable attorney's fees.

    c) In the event of an Infringement Claim Subcontractor will, with the consent of Prime: 
       i) obtain the rights to use the infringing material; 
       ii) modify the Deliverables so as to render them non-infringing and functionally equivalent; or 
       iii) provide functionally equivalent substitute Deliverables; 

       provided, however, that if none of the other options set forth in this paragraph can reasonably be achieved, Subcontractor may, in its sole discretion, elect to refund to Prime all fees paid to Subcontractor under the applicable SOW in full satisfaction of Subcontractor's obligations under the SOW. Any remedy under this paragraph shall be undertaken at the expense of the party that furnished the infringing material.

    a) Prime warrants that Subcontractor's use of any and all materials furnished by Prime hereunder will not violate or conflict with any intellectual property rights of any third party including, but not limited to, copyrights, patent and trademarks.

    b) Subcontractor warrants that:

       i) it will perform the Work and all services to be rendered hereunder with that standard of care, skill and diligence normally provided by a professional person in the performance of similar services with respect to work similar to that specified by any SOW;

       ii) upon delivery of each Deliverable to Prime, Prime will have marketable title to that Deliverable, free and clear of all liens and encumbrances;

       iii) the Deliverables will meet the standards customarily met by professional persons providing such Deliverables and also any specifications set forth in the SOW applicable thereto;

       iv) Subcontractor, Subcontractor's employees, contractors or agents will not perform services, disclose Confidential Information or engage in any consultation work for a third party relating to Prime's current or anticipated business which would conflict with Subcontractor's obligations to Prime under this Agreement, without first obtaining Prime's prior written consent;

       v) the Deliverables and all work products created and delivered by Subcontractor hereunder will not violate or conflict with any intellectual property rights of any third persons including, but not limited to, copyrights, patents and trademarks.

    d) EXCEPT AS SET FORTH ABOVE, NEITHER PARTY MAKES ANY EXPRESS OR IMPLIED REPRESENTATIONS OR WARRANTIES INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY AND FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

12. **LIMITATION OF LIABILITY**

    a) Neither party shall be liable hereunder for special or consequential losses or damages of any kind or nature whatsoever, including but not limited to lost profits, lost records or data, lost savings, loss of use of facility or equipment, loss by reason of facility shut-down or non-operation or increased expense of operations, or other costs, charges, penalties, or liquidated damages, regardless of whether arising from breach of contract, warranty, tort, strict liability or otherwise, even if advised of the possibility of such loss or damage, or if such loss or damage could have been reasonably foreseen.

    b) Except as otherwise provided in Sections 6, 7, and 8, Subcontractor's liability hereunder, regardless of the form of action, shall not exceed the total amount paid for services under this Agreement. Subcontractor's liability shall not be so limited with respect to injuries to persons or damage to tangible property arising out of the negligence or willful misconduct of Subcontractor or its employees.
 
    c) Neither party's liability shall be limited by this section with respect to claims arising from breach of the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement or arising from such party's infringement or misappropriation of the other party's (or Prime's customer's) intellectual property rights.

13. **INSURANCE**

    a) Subcontractor shall carry and maintain for the term of this Agreement, at its own expense, insurance coverage adequate for the defense and indemnification of any and all claims, demands, or actions arising out of any actions taken by Subcontractor in the performance of Subcontractor's obligations under this contract, including general business liability insurance, and shall provide Prime with evidence of same upon request.

14. **SUBCONTRACTOR PERSONNEL**

    a) Subcontractor personnel who provide services to Prime under this Agreement may perform similar services for others during the term of this Agreement, with the exception of competitors of Prime. Subcontractor will make reasonable efforts to honor specific requests of Prime regarding assignment of Subcontractor personnel, but Subcontractor reserves the right to make and change all such assignments, provided that Prime shall retain the right to reject the specific personnel assigned to Projects.

15. **INDEPENDENT CONTRACTORS**

    a) Subcontractor and Prime shall at all times be independent parties. Neither party is an employee, joint-venturer, agent, or partner of the other, neither party is authorized to assume or create any obligations or liabilities, express or implied, on behalf of or in the name of the other. The employees, methods, facilities and equipment of each party shall at all times be under the exclusive direction and control of that party.

16. **NON-COMPETITION**

    a) In pursuing the activities contemplated under this Agreement, Subcontractor and Prime shall in all instances cooperate in good faith; neither party shall, in particular, undercut the other party's offering for services and deliverables vis-à-vis a customer which are similar in scope.

    b) If Prime introduced the customer opportunity, Subcontractor shall not, during the term of the Agreement and for twelve (12) months thereafter, solicit business from such customer in the territory in which Subcontractor and Prime cooperated under the Agreement nor shall Subcontractor directly or indirectly compete with Prime in the provision of the Work of the kind and nature Subcontractor performs for Prime pursuant to the Agreement.

17. **MISCELLANEOUS**

    a) **COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS.** At all times, Subcontractor shall comply with all laws, rules, ordinances, decrees and regulations (including, but not limited to, the necessary work permits/authorizations required for the performance of Work for Subcontractor Personnel) applicable to its activities under this Agreement. Subcontractor shall indemnify Prime for any costs, expenses, injury and damage caused to Prime as a result of Subcontractor's failure to comply with applicable laws, rules, ordinances, decrees and regulations.

    b) **PUBLICITY.** Neither party shall use the name of the other for any commercial purpose without the prior written consent of the other, provided that Prime may inform its customer that Subcontractor will be performing services on behalf of such customer.

    c) **ASSIGNMENT.** Subcontractor may not assign any rights or delegate any obligations created by this Agreement without the prior written consent of Prime, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld. Any assignment in violation of this Agreement is void. This Agreement shall be binding upon the heirs, successors, legal representatives and permitted assigns of the parties.

    d) **FORCE MAJEURE.** Neither party shall be considered in default in the performance of any obligation hereunder to the extent that the performance of such obligation is prevented or delayed by fire, flood, explosion, strike, war, insurrection, embargo, government requirement, civil or military authority, act of God, or any other event, occurrence or condition which is not caused, in whole or in part, by that party, and which is beyond the reasonable control of that party. The parties shall take all reasonable action to minimize the effects of any such event, occurrence or condition.

    e) **SEVERABILITY.** If any provision of this Agreement is found invalid or unenforceable by an arbitration panel or a court of competent jurisdiction, such provision will be narrowed (or deleted if necessary) to the minimum extent necessary to make such provision enforceable, and the remaining provisions of this Agreement shall continue in full force and effect.

    f) **RESERVATIONS OF RIGHTS.** A delay or failure in enforcing any right or remedy afforded hereunder or by law shall not prejudice or operate to waive that right or remedy or any other right or remedy, including any remedy for a future breach of this Agreement, whether of a like or different character.

    g) **ENTIRE AGREEMENT, AMENDMENTS AND WAIVER.** This Agreement, together with every SOW executed by the parties, constitutes the entire agreement of the parties concerning the subject matter hereof and thereof, superseding any and all previous agreements and understandings, whether oral or written, with respect to the subject matter of this Agreement. No representation or promise relating to and no amendment or modification of this Agreement will be binding unless it is in writing and signed by an authorized representative of each party. No waiver by a party of any breach of any provision of this Agreement will constitute a waiver of any other breach of that or any other provision of this Agreement.

    h) **NOTICE.** All notices will be given in writing and addressed to the party to whom it is intended at the address set forth on the first page of this Agreement. Either party may change its mailing address by written notice to the other party. The parties may communicate via electronic mail regarding the SOW, Project(s) and Deliverable(s), however, all formal notice must be in writing and sent to the other party as described in this section.

    i) **SURVIVAL.** The provisions of Sections 3, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 14 and 15 shall survive any expiration, cancellation or termination of this Agreement.
